Summer Walking Holiday Destinations

Planning your next summer walking adventure? Here's a concise guide to Norway, Scilly, Bavaria, the Dolomites, Montenegro, and Albania, outlining their unique appeal, walking characteristics, and a glimpse into a potential week-long itinerary.

1. Norway

Norway offers dramatic fjord landscapes and towering mountains, providing a sense of grandeur and wild beauty for walkers. Expect epic scenery and opportunities for truly remote experiences.

Walking Characteristics:

Trails range from coastal paths to steep mountain ascents. Weather is unpredictable, so layers are essential. Famous hikes like Pulpit Rock are demanding day trips.

Pros:

  • Epic fjord and mountain landscapes.
  • Sense of vast wilderness and solitude.
  • Midnight Sun in the north for extended daylight.
  • High standards of safety and cleanliness.

Cons:

  • Very high cost for accommodation, food, and transport.
  • Unpredictable weather, even in summer (rain, cooler temps).
  • Popular spots can be extremely crowded.

Potential 7-Day Plan Summary:

**Day 1-3 (Stavanger/Fjord Region):** Arrive in Stavanger, explore, and hike to Pulpit Rock. Optionally, a Lysefjord cruise or the challenging Kjeragbolten hike. Travel to Bergen.

2. Scilly Isles, UK

A unique archipelago off Cornwall, the Isles of Scilly offer gentle island-hopping walks, pristine white sand beaches, and crystal-clear waters.

Walking Characteristics:

Primarily coastal paths, beaches, and quiet lanes. Terrain is gentle, suitable for all levels. Explore islands by walking around them and using inter-island boats.

Pros:

  • Unique island-hopping experience.
  • Stunning beaches and clear waters.
  • Mild climate, often warmer than mainland UK.
  • Peaceful and secluded atmosphere.
  • Pedestrian-friendly with minimal cars.

Cons:

  • No challenging mountain hiking.
  • Weather can be changeable (typical British summer).
  • Higher cost for travel and accommodation.
  • Fewer amenities on smaller islands.

Potential 7-Day Plan Summary:

**Day 1-2 (St. Mary's):** Arrive on St. Mary's, explore Hugh Town, and walk the island's coastal path.

**Day 3-6 (Island Hopping):** Day trips by boat to St. Martin's, Tresco (Abbey Garden), St. Agnes (Gugh), and Bryher for diverse walks and scenery.

**Day 7:** Final island breakfast and departure.

3. Bavaria, Germany

Bavaria offers a delightful mix of rolling hills, deep forests, charming villages, and the majestic German Alps, blending nature, culture, and comfort.

Walking Characteristics:

Trails are well-marked, from gentle riverside walks to moderate alpine hikes. Paths lead through meadows, past lakes, and to viewpoints, often accessible from picturesque towns.

Pros:

  • Excellent infrastructure and well-maintained trails.
  • Combines nature with charming culture and iconic castles.
  • Variety of terrain suitable for diverse fitness levels.
  • Enjoy hearty Bavarian cuisine and world-famous beers.

Cons:

  • Popular areas, especially around castles, can be very crowded.
  • Generally more developed and less 'wild' than some other options.

4. Dolomites, Italy

A UNESCO World Heritage site, the Dolomites are famous for their unique, pale, jagged peaks that glow pink at sunset, offering a blend of rugged alpine terrain and comfortable infrastructure.

Walking Characteristics:

Well-maintained trails from easy strolls to challenging multi-day treks and via ferratas. Paths lead to rifugios (mountain huts) for food and overnight stays.

Pros:

  • Truly iconic and unique mountain landscapes.
  • Excellent infrastructure with well-marked trails and rifugios.
  • Variety of trails for all fitness levels, including via ferratas.
  • Delicious Italian and Austrian-influenced cuisine.

Cons:

  • Can get very busy and crowded, especially in peak season.
  • Generally more expensive than Eastern European destinations.
  • Afternoon thunderstorms are common in summer.

Potential 7-Day Plan Summary:

**Day 1-3 (Cortina d'Ampezzo Area):** Arrive, explore Cortina, hike Cinque Torri & WWI museum. Trek the iconic Tre Cime di Lavaredo loop.

**Day 4-7 (Cortina/Val Gardena Area):** Visit Lake Braies and Prato Piazza. Optional via ferrata or Tofana di Mezzo cable car. Day trip to Val Gardena for Seceda views before departure.

5. Montenegro

Montenegro is a country of striking contrasts, from its dramatic Adriatic coastline to rugged, pristine mountains, an emerging destination for walkers seeking adventure and untouched beauty.

Walking Characteristics:

Diverse walking, from coastal paths along the Bay of Kotor to challenging trails in Durmitor and Prokletije National Parks. Path conditions vary; some hikes involve significant elevation gain.

Pros:

  • Stunning diversity: mountains, coast, lakes, historical towns.
  • More affordable than Western European destinations.
  • Opportunities for true wilderness and solitude.
  • Emerging destination with a sense of discovery.

Cons:

  • Developing infrastructure (transport, some trails).
  • Popular coastal towns can be very busy in summer.
  • Limited English proficiency outside major tourist hubs.

Potential 7-Day Plan Summary:

**Day 1-3 (Kotor Area):** Arrive in Kotor, hike city walls, explore Old Town. Hike the Ladder of Kotor and visit Perast. Day trip to Lovcen National Park.

**Day 4-7 (≈Ωabljak/Durmitor):** Travel to ≈Ωabljak, walk Black Lake. Hike in Durmitor NP (e.g., Bobotov Kuk or shorter trails). Visit Tara River Canyon before departure.

6. Albania

Albania offers a truly authentic and adventurous walking holiday experience, especially in its mountainous north ("Accursed Mountains"), a rugged and incredibly beautiful wilderness.

Walking Characteristics:

Challenging, remote trails, often connecting traditional villages (e.g., Valbona to Theth). Trail marking can be rudimentary; local guides are often recommended.

Pros:

  • Truly wild and untouched mountain wilderness.
  • Very affordable destination.
  • Authentic cultural immersion and warm hospitality.
  • Ideal for adventurous, off-the-beaten-path hikers.

Cons:

  • Least developed tourism infrastructure (roads, services).
  • Potential language barrier; English less common.
  • Challenging trails requiring good fitness.
  • Very hot in coastal/southern regions in summer.
